We live in a world of chronic stress where troubles encircle your life from the moment you wake up in the morning to the time you go back to sleep. Every other person is depressed or suffering from anxiety. The term has become so sommon that anxiety disorders are no news to people around you. It is so common that people often ignore these conditions.


On the other hand, many people live in denial and fail to appreciate the gravity of the condition. They think that a person suffering from anxiety is faking it. These misconceptions are simple present due to a lack of awareness.




What are anxiety disorders?


Anxiety as we know it is a physologiecal reaction to stress. It can be beneficial in some conditions where it pushes the body to work at its maximum capacity. It helps our body to be prepared and alert so that it is able to give an immediate response to the problems. However, chronic anxiety can lead to an excited to an state of mind.


It pushes your brain into overdrive. This deranges the primary brain functions, compromising your thinking capacity. Almost 30% of people in the world today suffer from anxiety disorders. However, many of these are treatable. the problem is they need to be identified first in order to be treated. So, what is axienty?


Anxiety is a fear or concern about the future. The prospect of the events to come in the near future frighten a person so that they are unable to think rationally.

Types of Stress Disorders


There are a number of divisions of stress disorders. Here we divide stress disorders into 8 types. Each anxiety disorders has its own specific and quality that makes is special and unique. Every patient has a different pathological procces behind the problem, which also alters the treatment protocol needed to treat each disorder.
